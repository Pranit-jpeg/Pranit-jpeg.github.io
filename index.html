From the desk

I work on applied empirical economics — labor markets, education policy, public finance, and the corners of macro where demographics and real rates are still being argued out. The part I find most interesting sits one layer below the regression: the sample-period break nobody flags, the vintage of the data, the identifying assumption that only holds in calm quarters. I like econometric honesty, well-labeled axes, and the parts of a policy memo most people skip.
