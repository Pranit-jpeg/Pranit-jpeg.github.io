Policy Evaluation

  • Fellowship · City of Boston DoIT Sep–Dec 2025

    Fare-free bus pilot & Family Days — two DiD frameworks for the City of Boston

    As Research & Evaluation Fellow at the City’s Department of Innovation and Technology: a difference-in-differences evaluation of the fare-free MBTA bus pilot across 25 routes, using 1M+ stop-level ridership observations (2017–2024); a separate triple-interaction DiD on Boston Family Days participation to test for chilling effects in non-English-speaking and East Boston households.

Applied Econometrics

  • Replications · Identification 2025–26

    Four canonical identification papers, replicated end-to-end

    Card (1990) on the Mariel Boatlift and immigrant wage effects; Dube, Lester & Reich on minimum-wage spillovers at state borders; Thistlethwaite & Campbell (1960) — the original regression-discontinuity paper; plus the Basten & Betz (2013) fuzzy spatial RDD. One folder, each as a self-contained notebook with the data, the specification, and a short interpretation of the result.

  • Econometrics · Replication writeup 2025

    Religious composition and political outcomes: a fuzzy spatial RDD replication

    A standalone six-page writeup of the Basten & Betz (2013) replication, framed as a writing sample. First-stage discontinuity 0.393 (SE 0.014) across 192 municipalities, R² = 0.949; includes 2SLS estimates and an explicit discussion of local randomisation, continuity, and instrument validity.

Structural Econometrics & IO

  • Structural · Demand Estimation 2026

    BLP & Nevo — random-coefficients discrete-choice demand on differentiated products

    The canonical Berry-Levinsohn-Pakes (1995) random-coefficients logit demand system, implemented alongside a nested logit and a Nevo-style (2000) application to the ready-to-eat cereal market. The set includes a plain random-utility baseline, a nested logit, the full BLP estimator, and the Nevo instrument strategy — the four notebooks that together map the standard IO demand-estimation toolkit.

Machine Learning & Unsupervised Methods

  • Coursework · DS5230 Northeastern 2025–26

    An unsupervised-learning portfolio — from custom DBSCAN to topic models

    Twelve notebooks covering the standard unsupervised toolkit and a few non-standard ones: a from-scratch DBSCAN paired with UMAP on MNIST, FashionMNIST, and 20 Newsgroups; KMeans, GMM, and the EM algorithm implemented by hand; t-SNE dimensionality reduction; LDA and NMF topic modeling on news corpora; Gibbs and MCMC sampling; collaborative filtering on MovieLens; Girvan–Newman community detection on academic citation networks.

    JobPipeline — a local machine for filtering noise
