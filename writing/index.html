  • Review & Critique · Macro 8 pages

    Review of Carvalho, Ferrero, Mazin & Nechio (2025) — demographics and real interest rates

    A structured critique of CFMN’s open-economy life-cycle model and panel ECM. Main concerns: Θ is not exogenous in the way the empirics assume (it co-moves with the savings glut, safe-asset shortage, and Basel II); the 1.45 long-run elasticity on the global rate is mechanically biased by a weighted-average-of-the-LHS construction; the Kao test is invalid under cross-sectional dependence. Three named extensions: EU accession as staggered DiD, China’s WTO entry as an integration shock, pension reforms as IV.

  • Referee Report · Labor / Public 2 pages

    Comment on Garrett, Ohrn & Suárez Serrato (2020) — tax policy and local labor markets

    A tight referee-style read of the authors’ DiD evaluation of federal bonus depreciation. Flags SUTVA violations from commuting and firm reallocation; the two-way fixed-effects with continuous treatment problem; the absence of heterogeneity analysis across industries and worker types; and the distributional implications of the employment–earnings disconnect.

  • Replication · Applied Econometrics 6 pages

    Religious composition and political outcomes: a fuzzy spatial RDD replication

    Replicates Basten & Betz (2013) with a fuzzy spatial regression discontinuity design using the historical Protestant–Catholic border in Switzerland as an instrument for Protestant share. First-stage coefficient 0.393 (SE 0.014) across 192 municipalities; includes reduced-form plots, 2SLS estimates, and an explicit discussion of local randomisation, continuity, and instrument validity.
